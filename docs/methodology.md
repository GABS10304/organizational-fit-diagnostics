# Methodology

## Overview
Organizational Fit Diagnostics uses three dimensions to assess organizational health and fit:
- **Strategy** – how well the organization aligns its direction with market reality
- **Team** – how effectively teams operate, decide, and grow
- **Culture** – how the organization handles learning, collaboration, and change

## Scoring
Each dimension is scored as the average of its constituent survey questions (1–10 scale).

| Level  | Score Range |
|--------|-------------|
| Low    | 1.0 – 3.9   |
| Medium | 4.0 – 6.9   |
| High   | 7.0 – 10.0  |

## Largest Gap Area
The dimension with the lowest score is flagged as the **Largest Gap Area** — the most critical focus point for interventions.

## Survey Dimensions

### Strategy
- Market dynamics
- Transformation pressure
- Strategy adjustment frequency
- Customer responsiveness
- Industry competitiveness
- Innovation importance
- Regulatory anticipation
- Digital maturity

### Team
- Team autonomy
- Role clarity
- Priority change frequency
- Skills development
- Data usage in decisions
- Efficiency vs. innovation balance

### Culture
- Cross-department collaboration
- Mistake handling
- Decision transparency
- Learning promotion
- Technology adaptability
- Collaboration culture

## Design principles
- Anonymous by default — no personal data required.
- Lightweight — usable in a 30-minute workshop.
- Neutral — does not prescribe agile or any specific methodology.
- Iterative — designed to be re-run after 30–90 days.

## Theoretical basis
The framework draws on:
- Evidence-Based Management (EBM) principles
- Organizational learning theory (Argyris, Schön)
- Psychological safety research (Edmondson, 1999)
- Self-Determination Theory (Deci & Ryan)
